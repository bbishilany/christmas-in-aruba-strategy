Section 01

Tracking Setup

Before any ad spend begins, two foundational tracking systems need to be in place. Both are free, both use Shopify's native tools, and both are non-negotiable prerequisites for running paid campaigns.

Meta Pixel: Full E-Commerce Tracking

What needs to happen

We need to complete the connection between the Shopify store and the existing Meta Pixel. Today, the Pixel is only tracking pageviews — proper e-commerce tracking is not implemented on the site.

Once connected, every key action a visitor takes — viewing a product, adding to cart, starting checkout, or completing a purchase — will be sent to Meta automatically. Meta's algorithm needs this data to identify more potential buyers and reduce wasted spend.

Google Analytics 4

What needs to happen

We need to install Google Analytics on the store. This provides a separate, independent view of store performance — where visitors come from, what they do, and what is driving sales. This is the foundation for future reporting.

Section 03

Meta Ads Advertising

What Happened Before

The account ran one test campaign in May 2026 with a $128 total budget. It generated traffic but zero measurable sales — not because the product lacked interest, but because the tracking was broken. Meta's algorithm never received a signal of who actually purchased, so it had no way to identify more buyers.

The Strategy: B2C Consumer Campaign

Consumer Campaign (B2C)
$25/day

Targeting Aruba residents who are likely to decorate their homes for Christmas. The campaign leads with the pre-order payment offer: "Reserve now, 4 easy payments, guaranteed delivery before November." Splitting into installments directly addresses the price-sensitive nature of the Aruba market — the barrier is not interest, it's upfront cost. Pre-order creates urgency and locks in commitment before inventory is allocated.

Creative Approach

Video is the priority. The previous test confirmed this — videos drove significantly higher engagement than static images. A small set of static images will complement the campaign for placements where video does not apply.

Section 04

Add to Cart as Our Success Metric

Why This Matters

Christmas in Aruba is a high-ticket seasonal opportunity in a small market. "Add to Cart" represents the moment when a visitor places a product in their shopping cart — one step before purchase.

We use this as the primary success signal because completed purchases will naturally be limited to a few per week, which is not enough data for Meta's algorithm to properly learn who the best buyers are.

Add to Cart events happen more frequently while still representing strong purchase intent. Someone does not add an Afl 700 Christmas tree to their cart by accident. By optimizing for this action, we give Meta the volume of data it needs to identify the right audience and improve performance throughout the pre-order season.
